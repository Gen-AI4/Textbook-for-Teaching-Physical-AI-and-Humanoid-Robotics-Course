# Chapter 1 - Introduction to Physical AI

## Overview

This chapter introduces the concept of Physical AI - a field that bridges traditional artificial intelligence with physical systems. Unlike conventional AI that operates primarily in digital spaces, Physical AI involves AI systems that interact directly with the physical world through sensors and actuators.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:
- Distinguish Physical AI from traditional AI approaches
- Understand the fundamental challenges of implementing AI on physical systems
- Identify key applications of Physical AI in robotics
- Explain why Physical AI is relevant to humanoid robotics

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI represents a paradigm shift from traditional AI approaches that primarily process information in digital spaces. In Physical AI, the AI system is embedded in or directly connected to physical systems, creating tight coupling between computation and physical reality.

### Key Characteristics

1. **Embodied Computation**: The AI system is physically embedded in the environment it operates in
2. **Real-time Interaction**: Processing must occur in time with physical dynamics
3. **Uncertainty Management**: Physical systems introduce real-world uncertainties
4. **Safety-Critical Operations**: Decisions directly affect physical systems and potentially humans

## Connection to Humanoid Robotics

Physical AI is particularly relevant to humanoid robotics because:
- Humanoid robots must operate in human environments
- The AI must process real sensor data and generate real actuator commands
- Safety is paramount when the AI controls physical systems near humans
- The form factor of humanoid robots makes them suitable for human-centric environments

## Summary

This chapter has introduced the fundamental concepts of Physical AI and its relevance to humanoid robotics. In the next chapter, we'll explore how the physical nature of systems affects sensing and perception.
